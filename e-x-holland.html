Keukenhof Garden
Date of organization

Keukenhof - the world's largest tulip garden is a top attraction for Dutch people as well as tourist followers around the world with the beauty of hundreds of flowers every spring.And it is open to tourists starting from the last week of March to mid-May.

Festival Details

This is the world's largest flower garden with an area of 32 hectares and about 7 million tulips of 100 different varieties.


This flower garden is the idea that appeared in 1949 of the then mayor of Lisse. The idea was to create an exhibition that florists from all over the Netherlands and Europe could participate in.


Keukenhof is named "Garden of Europe", because since its founding, it is the world's largest spring flower garden in the Northern Hemisphere. The best time to see tulips is around mid-April, depending on the weather.



Although the Keukenhof flower garden only welcomes tourists for 2 months, it is estimated that there are millions of visitors to enjoy each event. According to statistics, up to now, the world's largest tulip garden in the Netherlands has received more than 50 million visitors, of which 75% are international visitors from 100 different countries.



A special thing is that the flowers at Keukenhof Park are displayed in 5 zones named after Dutch kings and queens, Oranje Nassau, Wilhemina, Julinana, Beatrix and Willem Alexander. The three main booths are the place that attracts a lot of tourists because there are 30 most unique types of flowers and ornamental plants that are personally selected by the Expert Council.




Not only tulips, the paradise of colors and fragrances Dutch Keukenhof flower garden also gathers many other beautiful flowers. First, there are 15,000 lily flowers with more than 300 different varieties, followed by clusters of white and yellow daffodils, purple heart hyacinths peeking under the ancient oak trees.




In the gentle fragrance of hundreds of flowers, you can stroll along the aisles to admire and relax. You can also stop at a certain chair, inhale the scent of flowers and receive the gentle spring sunshine.
